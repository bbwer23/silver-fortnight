Post-Encounter Guide

The encounter has ended. Here is your roadmap for protecting your legal interests.

1. Immediate Documentation

Write down everything while it is fresh. Include badge numbers, car numbers, the exact time, and the names of any witnesses. Do not post this on social media.

2. Preservation of Evidence

If you recorded the incident, back up the footage to two separate cloud services. If you have physical injuries, take high-resolution photos immediately.

3. The "Translator" - Key Legal Terms

Reasonable Suspicion: A lower standard. Allows for a brief "stop and frisk" for weapons.
Probable Cause: A higher standard. Required for an actual arrest or a full vehicle search without consent.

4. Filing a Complaint

Contact the Internal Affairs (IA) department of the agency involved. You have the right to request body-worn camera (BWC) footage via a Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) request.
